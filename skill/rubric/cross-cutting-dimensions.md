# Cross-Cutting Dimensions

These nine dimensions are skills that show up across the entire call, not at any single stage. Each one gets its own 1–10 score, with evidence drawn from across the transcript.

A rep can score well on individual stages while scoring poorly on cross-cutting dimensions — for example, a rep who hits each stage's checkpoint but never adapts to the prospect's energy is failing at mirroring even though their stage scores look fine. The dimensions catch what the stage rubric misses.

## Universal scoring anchors

Same as for stages:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **1–2** | Skill absent or executed in a way that damaged the call |
| **3** | Skill attempted but executed weakly |
| **4–5** | Skill present but with significant gaps |
| **6** | Competent execution |
| **7–8** | Strong execution; pattern visible across the call |
| **9** | Excellent; rare execution |
| **10** | Exceptional; reserve for teaching examples |

---

## Dimension 1: Qualifying depth

**What you are scoring:** how deeply the rep dug into the prospect's situation across the entire qualifying section (asks + needs + yesterdays). This is partially redundant with the stage scores but evaluates the *cumulative* depth — a rep can score 6 on each individual stage and still have shallow overall qualifying.

### What good looks like
- Multiple follow-up questions per topic, each going deeper
- Status, outcome, and consequence questions all present
- Emotional why surfaced explicitly
- Specific past attempts with duration and reasons for failure
- Patterns articulated by the prospect, not projected by the rep

### Failure modes
- Surface-level questions only
- One layer of follow-up at most
- Prospect's emotional content not explored
- Past attempts handled abstractly
- Rep talking more than the prospect during qualifying

### Scoring signal
Count the ratio of rep talking to prospect talking during the first 15 minutes. If the rep is talking more than the prospect during qualifying, the depth is almost certainly insufficient regardless of question quality.

## Dimension 2: Identity contrast construction

**What you are scoring:** did the rep build a clear gap between who the prospect is now and who they want to become, and did that gap get used later in the call?

### What good looks like
- Yesterdays produced concrete material about the prospect's current trajectory
- Future image (in the diagram) was specific to the prospect's stated identity
- The contrast between current and desired self was made explicit at some point
- The accountability metaphor (Netflix / human nature dip) landed because the prospect's yesterdays already established the recurring failure pattern

### Failure modes
- Yesterdays gathered but generic ("I've tried stuff before")
- Future image painted abstractly ("you'll feel better")
- No explicit contrast moment in the call
- The diagram delivered without callbacks to yesterdays

### Specific things to look for
- Did the rep say something like "this time will be different because…" with a specific reason rooted in what the prospect just said?
- Did the diagram's results phase use details from the prospect's life (their kids, their wedding, their hobby)?
- Did the human nature dip moment land — did the prospect verbally agree this was their pattern?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires a clear, specific contrast that gets used in the diagram. A 5 is contrast attempted but vague. Below 5 if no identity contrast was constructed.

## Dimension 3: Label-and-confirm discipline

**What you are scoring:** did the rep label the prospect's situation back to them and get explicit verbal confirmation, and did they do this consistently throughout the call (not just at the needs stage)?

### What good looks like
- The needs stage label is clean, in the rep's words, with explicit confirmation
- Subsequent labels appear when warranted ("so what I'm hearing is…", "it sounds like…")
- Each label is followed by "is that right?" or equivalent
- When the prospect corrects, rep adjusts and re-confirms
- Confirmations are verbal and explicit, not assumed

### Failure modes
- Single label-and-confirm at needs, then no further labeling for the rest of the call
- Labels offered but no pause for confirmation
- Rep talks past the prospect's response
- Confirmation softened into rhetorical questions where any response would be taken as agreement
- Rep labels using the prospect's exact words rather than reframing

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires consistent label-and-confirm discipline across the call, with at least one clean execution at the needs stage. A 5 is label-and-confirm executed once but inconsistently elsewhere. Below 5 if the pattern was largely absent.

## Dimension 4: Mirroring and adaptation

**What you are scoring:** did the rep read this specific prospect and adjust their pace, energy, language, and depth accordingly?

### What good looks like
- Rep matches prospect's pace (slowing down for hesitant prospects, keeping energy up for animated ones)
- Rep adapts language to the prospect's level (technical vs. plain, casual vs. formal)
- Rep reads emotional cues and validates before pushing forward
- Rep adjusts depth — pushes deeper with engaged prospects, lighter with guarded ones
- Rep's rhythm shifts based on prospect's responses

### Failure modes
- Rep delivers high-energy storytelling to a guarded, analytical prospect
- Rep stays clinical with a warm, social prospect who wants connection
- Rep does not slow down when the prospect shows hesitation or vulnerability
- Rep runs the same pace and rhythm regardless of prospect's responses
- Rep ignores emotional content the prospect surfaces

### Specific things to look for
- When the prospect surfaces something vulnerable, did the rep validate before pushing forward?
- Did the rep's energy match the prospect's, or run independently of it?
- Did the prospect open up over the course of the call, or stay guarded?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires visible adaptation across the call — different pace and approach as the prospect's state shifts. A 5 is competent baseline rapport without active adaptation. Below 5 if the rep ran their default style regardless of prospect.

The four archetypes (Social, Analytical, Supportive, Independent) are a teaching scaffold, not a scoring requirement. Do not score whether the rep "correctly classified" the archetype. Score whether they read and adapted.

## Dimension 5: Callback discipline

**What you are scoring:** did the rep treat the qualifying material as ammunition — referencing it later in the call to land specific moves — or did they collect it as a checklist and never use it?

### What good looks like
- Yesterdays material referenced during the diagram ("unlike last time when you tried X…")
- Emotional why referenced during the future image ("imagine being able to keep up with your kids the way you said you wanted to")
- Pre-frame guarantees tied to specific gaps the prospect surfaced ("the custom workout matters because you said you didn't know what to do last time")
- If objections appeared, callbacks used in handling ("you mentioned earlier that consistency was the issue — that's exactly what this addresses")
- The diagram tied to the specific prospect, not delivered generically

### Failure modes
- Qualifying gathered, then never referenced
- Diagram delivered generically (the same diagram for any prospect)
- Future image painted in abstract terms not tied to the prospect's stated identity
- Objection handling reaches for generic responses instead of using the prospect's own words

### Specific things to look for
- Count the explicit callbacks — moments where the rep references something the prospect said earlier
- Were the callbacks specific (using actual details the prospect provided) or abstract (generalizing what the prospect said)?
- Did the diagram contain at least 2–3 specific references to this prospect's situation?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires multiple specific callbacks across the call. A 5 is one or two generic callbacks. Below 5 if qualifying material was effectively orphaned — gathered but not used.

This is one of the most reliable signals of a skilled rep. Newer reps collect discovery as a ritual; experienced reps treat it as ammunition. The score on this dimension often correlates strongly with whether the call closed.

## Dimension 6: Value communication

**What you are scoring:** did the rep communicate value through stories, metaphors, third-party framing, and timeframe construction — or did they default to feature-and-benefit lists?

### What good looks like
- Stories grounded in specific scenes the prospect can mentally inhabit
- Metaphors used to make abstract concepts tangible (the body as a house, accountability as Netflix, the Disneyland exhaustion)
- Third-party authority invoked at value-claim moments ("from the hundreds of clients I've worked with…")
- Timeframe construction (12-month arcs, "by month six," "a year from now") rather than today's-price framing
- Specific moments where the prospect can be heard reacting emotionally to the stories or metaphors

### Failure modes
- Feature lists ("we have customized workouts, meal plans, biometric scans")
- Generic transformation language ("you'll feel better, have more energy")
- No metaphors used
- No third-party authority — claims made by the rep without external grounding
- No timeframe construction — the prospect is buying a today's-price decision instead of a year of becoming someone different

### Specific things to look for
- Were there at least 2–3 stories/metaphors used during the diagram?
- Did the rep invoke "most members" or "from clients I've worked with" social proof?
- Did the rep paint a future scene with specific details from the prospect's life?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires consistent use of stories, metaphors, and social proof through the value communication phase. A 5 is some elements present but executed flatly. Below 5 if value was communicated as feature lists.

## Dimension 7: Conviction and tone

**What you are scoring:** did the rep deliver with confidence, or did they hedge, apologize, and undercut their own claims?

### What good looks like
- Direct statements without hedging ("this is what we do" not "I think we kind of do this")
- Price stated cleanly, without apology or pre-emptive justification
- Silences held, not filled
- Belief in the product audible in word choice and structure
- No verbal tics that signal nervousness ("um, like, you know" stacking)

### Failure modes
- Hedging language throughout ("I think," "kind of," "probably")
- Apologetic price reveal ("I know this might sound like a lot, but…")
- Silences filled with re-pitching or justification
- Pre-emptive defenses against objections that haven't appeared
- Verbal tics indicating nervousness, especially during high-stakes moments (price, close)

### Specific things to look for
- During the price reveal, was there hedging or apology?
- During the close, did the rep stay silent or fill the pause?
- Did the rep's word choice change between qualifying (where it can be more conversational) and the close (where it should be cleaner and more direct)?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires direct delivery with held silences and minimal hedging. A 5 is competent delivery with some hedging at high-stakes moments. Below 5 if hedging or apology dominated.

This dimension is harder to score from transcript alone because much of it is auditory. Score what's visible in word choice and structure, but note the limitation in your reasoning.

## Dimension 8: Momentum preservation

**What you are scoring:** once the prospect indicated agreement, did the rep move directly to logistics, or did they undercut their own close by reopening considerations?

### What good looks like
- After the prospect's "yes" or equivalent, rep moves to signing, payment method, or scheduling immediately
- No new variables introduced after the agreement
- No re-pitching after the close
- Energy stays high during the reinforce stage
- Contract walked through efficiently with reframes for any negatives

### Failure modes
- Rep gets the yes and continues to talk, reopening the prospect's evaluation
- Rep introduces a new feature or option after the prospect agreed
- Rep slows down at the moment of payment collection (signaling discomfort)
- Energy drops after the sale (signaling the rep was performing rather than genuinely excited)
- Rep over-explains the contract terms

### Specific things to look for
- After the prospect's first agreement, what did the rep say next?
- Did the rep introduce any new options, features, or considerations after the agreement?
- Did energy stay consistent or drop?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires clean transition to logistics after agreement, with no momentum-killing additions. A 5 is some momentum loss but recovered. Below 5 if the rep substantially undercut their own close.

This is one of the most preventable failure modes. New reps often kill their own closes by talking past the yes.

## Dimension 9: One-question-at-a-time discipline

**What you are scoring:** did the rep ask one question, pause, listen, and follow up — or did they stack questions?

### What good looks like
- Questions asked individually with pauses between
- Each question gets a substantive answer before the next is asked
- Follow-ups are responsive to what the prospect just said, not pre-loaded
- Silences after questions are tolerated

### Failure modes
- Question stacks ("so what's your goal, what have you tried, and why are you here?")
- Rep asks a question and immediately answers it themselves
- Rep asks a question and rephrases it before the prospect can answer
- Multi-part questions delivered as one ("what made you reach out, was it something specific, like did something happen, or is it more general?")

### Specific things to look for
- Count the question stacks across the call
- During qualifying specifically, how often did the rep stack questions?

### Scoring guidance
A 7+ requires consistent one-question discipline. A 5 is some stacking, especially under pressure. Below 5 if stacking was a frequent pattern.

This dimension correlates strongly with rep experience. New reps stack questions because they are nervous and trying to cover ground. Experienced reps trust the process and let one question land before asking another.

## Output requirement

For each cross-cutting dimension, the analyzer's JSON output must include:

```json
{
  "dimension": "callback_discipline",
  "score": 5,
  "evidence_quotes": [
    "Specific moments showing presence or absence of the skill"
  ],
  "pattern_observed": "Description of the pattern across the call",
  "highest_leverage_fix": "If this is a weakness, what specifically would change the dimension's score most"
}
```

The `highest_leverage_fix` field is for the dimensions where the rep scored 6 or below. For higher-scoring dimensions (7+), this field can be omitted or set to null.
